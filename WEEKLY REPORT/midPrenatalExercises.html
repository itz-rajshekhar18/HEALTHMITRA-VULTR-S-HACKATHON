Early Pregnancy Exercises

1. Cat-Cow Stretch

Benefits: This yoga stretch helps relieve tension in the lower back and improves flexibility in the spine, which is especially beneficial as your belly grows.

    How to do it:
  1. Start on all fours with your hands aligned under your shoulders and your knees under your hips.
  2. Inhale, arch your back, and look upward (Cow pose).
  3. Exhale, round your back, and tuck your chin toward your chest (Cat pose).
  4. Repeat for 10-12 gentle stretches.

2. Side-Lying Leg Lifts

Benefits: Strengthens the inner and outer thighs, hip muscles, and helps with pelvic stability.

    How to do it:
  1. Lie on your left side with your head supported by your arm.
  2. Keep your left knee slightly bent and extend your right leg straight.
  3. Lift your right leg to about hip height, then lower it back down slowly.
  4. Repeat 10-15 times on each side.

3. Wall Squats

Benefits: Strengthens the legs and glutes, and supports the growing belly by improving lower body stability.

    How to do it:
  1. Stand with your back against a wall, feet hip-width apart.
  2. Slowly slide down the wall until your knees are at a 90-degree angle.
  3. Hold for a few seconds, then slide back up.
  4. Aim for 10-12 repetitions.

4. Seated Pelvic Tilts

Benefits: Strengthens core muscles, eases lower back pain, and improves pelvic mobility.

    How to do it:
  1. Sit on an exercise ball or a chair with feet flat on the floor.
  2. Tilt your pelvis slightly forward and backward, feeling a gentle stretch in your lower back.
  3. Repeat for 10-15 reps.

5. Modified Plank

Benefits: Strengthens the core, back, and shoulders, providing support for your growing belly.

    How to do it:
  1. Start on all fours with hands aligned under shoulders.
  2. Walk your knees slightly back and extend your body into a plank with your knees on the ground.
  3. Hold for 10-15 seconds, gradually building up to 30 seconds if comfortable.

6. Arm and Shoulder Toning with Light Weights

Benefits: Helps build strength and endurance in the arms, which can be helpful for carrying the baby and improving posture.

    How to do it:
  1. Use 1-3 pound weights and perform exercises like bicep curls, shoulder presses, and lateral raises.
  2. Do 10-12 reps of each, resting as needed.

7. Bird Dog

Benefits: Improves core stability, strengthens the back, and helps with balance.

    How to do it:
  1. Start on all fours, with wrists under shoulders and knees under hips.
  2. Extend your right arm forward and your left leg back, keeping your back straight.
  3. Hold for a few seconds, then return to starting position and switch sides.
  4. Repeat for 8-10 reps on each side.

These exercises can help keep you active and manage pregnancy-related discomforts as you progress. Aim to perform them at a comfortable pace, and prioritize proper breathing and form throughout.
