Early Pregnancy Exercises

1. Walking

Benefits: Walking is one of the safest and easiest forms of exercise during pregnancy. It helps improve cardiovascular health and keeps you active without putting too much strain on your joints.

How to do it: Aim for 20-30 minutes of brisk walking at least 3-4 times a week. Make sure you wear comfortable shoes and stay hydrated.

2. Swimming or Water Aerobics

Benefits: Water supports the body’s weight, easing joint stress and reducing swelling. It provides a full-body workout and improves cardiovascular fitness.

How to do it: Swim or perform water aerobics for 20-30 minutes, 2-3 times a week. Avoid diving or jumping into the water.

3. Prenatal Yoga

Benefits: Yoga helps improve flexibility, reduces back pain, and encourages relaxation. It’s also a great way to connect with your body and prepare mentally for childbirth.

How to do it: Join a prenatal yoga class or follow an online routine. Focus on poses that open the hips, stretch the back, and promote relaxation.

4. Pelvic Tilts

Benefits: Strengthens the abdominal muscles and reduces lower back pain.

How to do it: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Tighten your abdominal muscles and tilt your pelvis slightly upward. Hold for a few seconds, then relax. Repeat 10-15 times.

5. Kegels

Benefits: Strengthens the pelvic floor muscles, which can help with bladder control and support the growing uterus.

How to do it: Tighten the muscles you’d use to stop the flow of urine, hold for 5 seconds, then release. Repeat 10-15 times, 2-3 times a day.

6. Light Strength Training

Benefits: Helps build muscle strength to support joints, improves posture, and reduces pregnancy-related aches.

How to do it: Use light weights (1-3 pounds) or resistance bands to perform exercises like bicep curls, tricep extensions, and shoulder presses. Avoid heavy lifting and consult your healthcare provider.
