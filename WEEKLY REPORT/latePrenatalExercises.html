Early Pregnancy Exercises

1. Pelvic Tilts

Purpose:

How to Do It: Stand with your back against a wall, bend knees slightly, and gently rock your pelvis back and forth. You can also perform these on hands and knees for a greater range of motion.

2. Squats

Purpose: Opens the pelvis and strengthens the legs.

How to Do It: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, keeping your back straight, and lower yourself down into a squat position as far as comfortable. Use support, such as a chair, for balance if needed.

3. Butterfly Stretch

Purpose: Opens the hips and relieves tension in the inner thigh muscles.

How to Do It:Sit on the floor with feet touching, knees bent outward, and gently press your knees toward the floor using your elbows.

4. Cat-Cow Stretch

Purpose: Eases lower back tension and promotes flexibility in the spine.

How to Do It:On hands and knees, alternate between arching your back (cat) and dropping your belly while lifting your head and tailbone (cow).

5. Kegel Exercises

Purpose: Strengthens pelvic floor muscles, which support labor and recovery.

How to Do It: Squeeze and hold the muscles used to stop urine flow for a few seconds, then release.
